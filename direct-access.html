Direct Access Barristers

Following a change in the traditional referral rules in 2004, it has been possible for individuals, businesses, charities and other organisations to instruct barristers directly; this is known as Direct Public Access.

Not all cases are suitable for Direct Access and the circumstances of each need to be carefully considered. To find out if your case is suitable, please contact a member of Chambers' dedicated Direct Access clerking team on 0191 232 1927 who will discuss your case further and if appropriate arrange a consultation with a specialist barrister.

Please see the detailed FAQs section below for further information on the Direct Access process.

Instructing a Direct Access Barrister – FAQs

The majority of Trinity's barristers are qualified and experienced in dealing with Direct Access work across a wide range of practice areas including employment, family, immigration, planning, property, criminal, and many more. Please contact our Direct Access clerking team to discuss your specific matter and we will match you with the most appropriate barrister.
Fees vary depending on the nature of the work and the seniority of the barrister required. We are committed to transparency and will always provide a clear fee estimate before work commences. In many cases, Direct Access can be more cost-effective than instructing via a solicitor as there is no intermediary. Please contact our clerks for a fee estimate tailored to your specific matter.
Full information about the Direct Access scheme is available from the Bar Standards Board website. Our Direct Access clerking team is also happy to answer any questions you may have about the process and whether it is suitable for your case. You can reach them on 0191 232 1927 or by emailing info@trinitychambers.co.uk.
The process typically involves: (1) contacting our Direct Access clerking team to discuss your matter; (2) receiving an assessment of whether Direct Access is suitable for your case; (3) being matched with an appropriate barrister; (4) agreeing fees and signing a client care letter; (5) providing the barrister with all relevant documents; (6) attending a consultation and receiving advice. We will guide you through each step of the process.
Barristers instructed under Direct Access can provide legal advice, draft documents, and represent you in court. However, there are certain tasks that barristers are not permitted to do under the Direct Access rules, such as handling client money (other than for specific purposes) and conducting litigation in most circumstances. In some complex cases, it may still be advisable to instruct a solicitor. Our clerks and barristers will be honest with you about whether Direct Access is right for your case.

Urgent Deadlines

If you are considering instructing a barrister on a Direct Access basis and your case has potential deadlines, then please get in touch as soon as possible and be aware that if any such deadlines have passed or are extremely close, then we may not be able to help.
