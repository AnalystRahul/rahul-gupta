ABOUT TRINITY

About Trinity Chambers

Trinity Chambers is proud of its reputation as being one of the leading sets of barristers' chambers in the North of England.

Endorsed by the leading legal directories:

"All clients within the Chambers are extremely friendly and will try to accommodate any specific requests. There is often a wide selection of Counsel available and they are happy to take the time to discuss the needs of individual cases and advise on Counsel they consider most suited. They are prompt in responding to communications and will forward on information which they consider may be of interest." Legal 500
"An excellent chambers and clerking service: they are always attentive and can offer a very wide range of expertise. The clients are very approachable and user-friendly, readily available and efficient with invoicing. You really feel you know where you stand with them." Chambers & Partners

This is a reputation we have won by investing in staff, services and facilities, by implementing a robust administration and clerking structure, and crucially, by supplying high quality legal advice and advocacy.

Trinity was the first chambers north of London, and only the fourth in the country, to be awarded the General Council of the Bar's BarMark involving an annual review of all aspects of practice management, client care and regulatory compliance as assessed and reported upon by the Bar Standards Institution. Trinity Chambers has the Cyber Essentials Plus accreditation.

Since our establishment in 1954, we have consistently grown so that we now offer a broad spectrum of specialisation, expertise and experience, with consistently high quality across the board.

Trinity Chambers is a trading name of Trinity Chambers Services Ltd, a Private Company Limited by Guarantee Registered in England No: 15846284. Registered Office Address: The Custom House, 39 Quayside, Newcastle Upon Tyne, England, NE1 3DE.

Facilities

Trinity Chambers provides the following facilities:

  • In Trinity (Newcastle) there are 7 conference rooms with a capacity of 4 to 12 people that are available in and out of published working hours. All rooms have internet access and telephone. Trinity (Teesside) has 1 conference room. These rooms, subject to availability, can be hired at a nominal rate through Chambers' Chief Executive.
  • Conferences can be arranged through the clerks at an appropriate venue and time.
  • Trinity's premises in Newcastle, Teesside and Leeds have audio conferencing facilities. Trinity (Newcastle) also has a video conferencing suite with seating for either 8 or 30 people. These facilities can be hired through the clerks (info@trinitychambers.co.uk) or 0191 232 1927.
  • Trinity (Newcastle) has seminar rooms with a capacity for 40 people.

Chambers' Policies

Trinity Chambers operates to the following policy undertakings:

Trinity Chambers is committed to equality and diversity in all aspects of its work. We do not discriminate on the grounds of age, disability, gender reassignment, marriage and civil partnership, pregnancy and maternity, race, religion or belief, sex, or sexual orientation. Our Equality and Diversity policy is reviewed annually and applies to all members of Chambers, pupils, employees and those seeking to join Chambers.
Trinity Chambers is opposed to all forms of unlawful discrimination. We are committed to promoting racial equality and diversity within Chambers. We recognise that discrimination may be direct or indirect, conscious or unconscious, and we take all reasonable steps to ensure that our practices do not disadvantage individuals from any racial group.
All barristers at Trinity Chambers are regulated by the Bar Standards Board and are subject to the Code of Conduct of the Bar of England and Wales. Our barristers provide independent, specialist legal advice and advocacy. We are committed to providing high quality, cost-effective legal services to all clients, whether instructed by solicitors or directly under the Direct Access scheme.
Trinity Chambers provides a wide range of general services to instructing solicitors and direct access clients. Our clerks team is available Monday to Friday, 8:30am to 6:00pm, to assist with all aspects of instructing counsel, including obtaining quotes, managing listings, and arranging conferences. Out-of-hours urgent assistance is available on 0191 245 0110.
We aim to respond to all enquiries within 24 hours on business days. For urgent matters, our clerks team will endeavour to respond as quickly as possible. Written advice and settled documents will be delivered within the agreed timescale. If any delay is anticipated, the instructing solicitor or client will be informed as soon as possible.
Trinity Chambers operates a professional clerking service with dedicated senior clerks for each of our three offices. Our clerks have extensive experience matching the right barrister to your case and ensuring the efficient running of your matter. All clerks are trained to the highest professional standards and are committed to providing an outstanding service.
Trinity Chambers is committed to transparency in all matters relating to fees and billing. Fee quotes are provided before work commences wherever possible. Fees are agreed with the relevant barrister and Chambers' clerks before instructions are accepted. Where fees cannot be fixed in advance, an estimate will be given. Invoices are issued promptly following the completion of work. Full details of our pricing structure are available from the clerks.
Trinity Chambers has a formal complaints procedure. If you are unhappy with any aspect of the service you have received, please contact our Chief Executive in the first instance. All complaints are investigated thoroughly and we aim to resolve them within 28 days. If you remain dissatisfied, you may refer your complaint to the Bar Standards Board or the Legal Ombudsman.
